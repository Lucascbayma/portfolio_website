Corrida de Sinais - Signal Race

The project was developed in the subject "Projetos 1", a very important college course to take
the first steps into the world of innovation.The main theme is Accessibility and Games, in which several groups
with disabilities were mentioned. My group and I had the honor of working with the group of hearing-impaired
people, who, unfortunately, have to face a major barrier in the world of physical and digital games. For this, we
developed a playful game with Arduino and RFID, initially designed for children, in which cards are available divided
by difficulties (Easy, Medium, and Hard) and categories (days of the week, numbers, objects, and animals) to facilitate
and increasingly enhance the interaction with people who belong to the mentioned group. During the presentation,
we could see how the audience interacted in such a positive way and, from a prototype, already learned a little more
about LIBRAS (Brazilian Sign Language).

Onde está meu bigode?

The game was developed by me and two other college friends in the subject "Introduction to Computing",
where we were able to explore the no-code game creation tool "Bitsy" and our artistic skills working with pixel art
and if-else mechanisms, where a room's door could only be opened when the key was found! The game features several
sprites and easter eggs about our professor from the subject. The goal is to find the main character's mustache, which
is a striking characteristic, and in the end, he is faced with an unexpected surprise!
